What is Prompt Science?

Prompt Science is an experimental editorial initiative exploring how humans and large language models (LLMs) can co-create scientific knowledge in a transparent, ethical, and reproducible way.

Mission

To promote a new mode of scientific production based on transparent, ethical, and reproducible collaboration between humans and large language models (LLMs) recognizing their potential to accelerate discovery, enhance creativity, and strengthen scientific integrity.

Vision

To become the leading global platform for humanâ€“LLM co-created scientific publications, establishing clear standards for interaction, documentation, and evaluation that drive a new era of open, explainable, and collaborative science.

Editorial Policies

  • Prompt Transparency: All contributions involving LLMs must include a prompt log describing the nature and role of the interactions.
  • Human Accountability: Human authors are fully responsible for content validity, originality, and ethical compliance.
  • Open Licensing: We support open science. All accepted contributions are published under a CC BY 4.0 license.
  • Numerical Results and Verification: Any numerical values, simulations, or quantitative results generated by LLMs must be independently verified through external tools or reproducible methods. Unverified numerical outputs should not be presented as scientific findings unless their verification or analysis is itself part of the study.

Disclosure of Humanâ€“LLM Collaboration

We ask all authors to provide a disclosure explaining how LLMs were used in their work. This includes:

  • Which model(s) were used, including provider and version.
  • Whether prompts were written by the human author, generated, or adapted from other sources.
  • Whether any of the text was generated entirely by the LLM, and if so, which parts.
  • If the human author edited, rejected, or rewrote suggestions by the LLM.

Being honest and precise in this disclosure helps build trust and transparency in our new modes of collaboration.

AI Contributor Disclosure Policy

Prompt Science is founded on a core principle: transparency in the use of large language models (LLMs). We recognize that researchers may vary in their familiarity with prompting practices and workflow integration, but all authors are expected to document their LLM usage in a clear, honest, and reproducible manner.

Minimum Disclosure Requirements

  • The main prompts used, or a representative example.
  • The model name and checkpoint (e.g., GPT-4, Claude Opus, Mistral 7B).
  • Key parameters such as temperature, top-p, etc.
